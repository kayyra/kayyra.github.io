Underworld Gods

the Underworld, also known as the realm of the dead, is called the "Inframundo" or "Hades." Here's a summary of the Underworld in Greek mythology: The Underworld is ruled by Hades, the god of the dead and wealth. It is a dark and gloomy realm located beneath the earth. When mortals died, their souls would journey to the Underworld, where they would be judged by Hades. The souls of the righteous and heroic would be rewarded with a peaceful afterlife in the Elysian Fields, a beautiful paradise. However, those who were wicked or had committed heinous crimes would be punished in Tartarus, a deep abyss of torment.

The Underworld is also home to various mythical creatures, such as the three-headed dog Cerberus, who guards the entrance, and the three judges of the dead, Minos, Rhadamanthus, and Aeacus. Additionally, the realm houses the River Styx, a boundary between the world of the living and the dead, which souls must cross to enter the Underworld. The Underworld plays a significant role in Greek mythology, with various myths and legends featuring heroes and gods venturing into Hades' realm in search of loved ones or to fulfill quests. It is a realm associated with death, judgment, and the afterlife in Greek mythology.
