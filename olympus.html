Olympus

From the union of the Titaness Rhea and the Titan Cronus, six gods were born, who would become the most important and prominent in Greek mythology. Uranus (the father of Cronus) had foretold that Cronus would suffer the same fate as him. Out of fear that this prophecy would come true, Cronus began devouring his children as they were born. However, when Rhea learned of this, she hid her last-born child, Zeus. Zeus was raised in a cave under the care of goats. When the time came, he confronted his father and made him drink a potion, causing Cronus to vomit up Zeus' siblings. Thus began the first Titanomachy, a war between the gods and the Titans.
